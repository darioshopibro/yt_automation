If you split your application into microservices, how do you get them all to talk to each other? There's three main ways that microservices can communicate. The first is by API. Each microservice has its own endpoint that the other microservices can call. The second option is to use a message broker. If what your application needs to do can be done asynchronously, then you're better off using a message broker. The last option is what we call a service mesh. A service mesh handles all of the communication as well as dealing with the discoverability and the reliability of your services. As we saw with monoliths, if you have one part of your application that's getting more requests, then you have no choice but to scale up your whole application. But with microservices, you can scale up just that one service and save money in the process.
