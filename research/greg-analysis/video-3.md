## SUMMARY

### Unique Transition Types Used:
1. **Pop/Scale with Spring** — Dominant. Elements 0→100% with overshoot bounce
2. **Path Tracing** — Dotted lines drawing themselves to connect nodes
3. **Morphing/Expansion** — Small elements expanding into large cards
4. **Physics Fall/Ingestion** — Elements drop downwards and are "absorbed" into another icon (robot head, FB logo) to represent data processing
5. **Continuous Slide (Infinite Scroll)** — Column of elements sliding to represent searching
6. **Fast-Counter** — Text numbers rapidly ticking up (timer, ad counter)

### Unique Element Types Used:
1. Badges/Pills
2. Code/Data Cards (with inner spinning gears)
3. App/Web Screenshots
4. Social Proof UI (Instagram comments)
5. Connecting Dotted Lines
6. Kinetic Typography

- Background: `#F4F4F4` (Off-White)
- Primary Dark: `#1E3A2F` (Deep Forest Green)
- Primary Light/Accent: `#82B596` / `#A8C2B0` (Soft Sage Green)
- Secondary Accent: `#DE6B58` (Muted Red)
- Cards: `#1A1A1A` (Near Black/Dark Grey)

### Timing:
- Average scene duration: **4-7 seconds**
- Average transition duration: **~0.5 seconds** (extremely tight, concurrent animations)
- Total video duration: **50 seconds**

### Overall Style:
This video continues the "spatial node-based canvas" style but leans heavier into "data visualization" animations — specifically using gravity falls and absorption effects to physically show raw data moving from scraping tools (Perplexity) into AI agents (Claude), and finally into deployment APIs (Facebook). It makes abstract digital processes feel tactile and mechanical.
